Crude malaria and SCD maps show observed burden directly and are sensitive to local population size and unstable small-area variation.
The crude local co-cluster proxy highlights areas where both malaria and SCD burdens are simultaneously high relative to the national distribution.
The Bayesian shared-risk map is smoother and more spatially coherent because it estimates the latent shared burden surface rather than reproducing raw local fluctuation.
Comparing crude and smoothed maps helps demonstrate why Bayesian disease mapping adds value: it reduces instability, clarifies broader shared spatial structure, and supports more defensible hotspot interpretation.
